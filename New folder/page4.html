Overview

GLOB~S is a platform for multi-disciplinary collaboration among scholars, business managers, and policymakers to generate science-based evidence of the drivers and consequences of business internationalization and to translate such evidence into performance- and social-welfare enhancing firm strategies, market practices, and public policy.

GLOB~S operates with four primary goals:

  1. To actively promote and develop academically rigorous and practically relevant research.                                                                                                                                                    We seek to generate and promote research that advances the scholarship on global strategy and organizational decision making while identifying managerial or policy actions to guide market and non-market strategy and enhance its societal benefits. 
  2. To develop and maintain firm and country level indices and databases.                      By developing and maintaining open-access measures of firm international expansion and country institutions and risk, we seek to disseminate informational resources that can be used by researchers and policy makers in their efforts to study and interpret the effects from the international expansion of firms.     
  3. To facilitate multi-sector collaboration. We foster projects that build the capacity of scholars to engage with business and public managers in understanding the firm and institutional determinants of the most pressing issues associated with the internationalization of the firm. Managers and policy can play an important role in guiding and developing questions that have practical implications while the findings from academic research can inform and influence firm decision making and the policy process.
  4. To advance education and training. We involve undergraduate and graduate students in the design and application of methodologies for the generation of cutting-edge scholarship.   

            To meet these goals, GLOB~S will connect academic researchers, corporate decision             makers and policy makers though workshops, conferences, sponsored faculty and             student research initiatives and the development of databases on firms, countries and             regions. We will build on the vibrant community of Washington D.C. while developing             cross-disciplinary and cross industry activities that integrate research, policy and             practical perspectives.
