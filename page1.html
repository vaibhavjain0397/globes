ABOUT US

Luis is at the faculty of the GW Business School where he teaches international business. He works studying the individual and institutional drivers of organizational decision making under risk and uncertainty and the consequences of such choices for firm performance and sustainability and economic development.  

LUIS BALLESTEROS

George Washington University, Co-Director

Heather Berry (PhD, UCLA) is an Associate Professor of International Business and International Affairs at the School of Business and the Elliott School of International Affairs at the George Washington University. Her research sits at the intersection of strategic management and international business and explores the global strategies, organizational structures and worldwide knowledge and innovation activities of multinational corporations.

HEATHER BERRY

George Washington University, Co-Director

"Bailey Keith is at the GW School of Business pursuing a BBA in International Business and a minor in International Affairs through the Elliott School, and is working to perfect her Spanish. A recipient of the Presidential Academic Scholarship, Bailey has interned at the State Department in the Bureau of Budget and Planning and on a Senate campaign where she conducted data analysis, budgeting and fund allocation, and strategic planning. She is a member of GW Women in Business and serves as the Leadership Chair on the executive board of her honors fraternity. At the Global Scope Research Lab, Bailey coordinates the project on how organizational behavior is impacted by manufactured disasters. This project tackles the topic of how social media can act as a catalyst for business responses to shocks, one of her major interests. Additionally, she has collaborated on the project that investigates the effects of uncertainty shocks on innovation where she has worked on processing and evaluating patent data. "
